=====================================================
Generated: 2026-03-13
Data: VIX and SPX daily OHLC from Yahoo Finance via yfinance
Database: market_data.db (SQLite)


1. METRIC DEFINITION
--------------------
"Max absolute SPX move from open" = max(|SPX_high - SPX_open|, |SPX_open - SPX_low|) / SPX_open * 100

This captures the largest percentage swing from the opening print in either
direction during the session. It answers: "Given VIX at the open, how far
could SPX move intraday?"

3. VIX ZERO-BASING
-------------------
Raw OLS produces a negative intercept (meaningless -- implies negative move at
VIX=0). We compute the x-intercept of each subset's OLS fit to find the
natural "VIX floor" that zeros the intercept:

  Sample             VIX Floor
  Full (1990+)          4.9
  2000+                 5.2
  Post Dot-Com (2004+)  5.0

The floor converges to ~5 across all subsets. This yields the clean formula:

  max_spx_move% = slope * (VIX_open - 5)

The 1990s slope is ~16% flatter than post-2000 eras, likely due to wider

6. VOL-OF-VOL: WHY QUADRATIC MATTERS
-------------------------------------
Residual standard deviation by VIX bucket (2004+):

  VIX Bucket   Residual Std
  10-15        0.33
  15-20        0.46
  20-25        0.60
  25-30        0.83

  40-45        1.11

The variance fans out ~6x from low VIX to crisis VIX. A linear model
underestimates tail risk at high VIX. The quadratic quantile regression
adds a (VIX-5)² term that curves the band upward, accounting for vol-of-vol.

8. COMPARISON TO ORIGINAL TUNNEL OF SORROW TOS SCRIPT
------------------------------------------------------
Original ThinkOrSwim formula:
  move = 0.000609 * (VIX - 8) + 0.0037
  move% = 0.0609 * (VIX - 8) + 0.37

Key differences:

  Parameter         TOS Script       Our Linear      Our Quad 75th
  Slope (%/vol)     0.0609           0.0700           0.0659 + curve
  VIX Floor         8                5                5
  Constant offset   +0.37%           0                +0.20%
  Containment       ~65%             ~59% (mean)      75%

  VIX   TOS Script   Our Mean    Our Quad 75th   Our Quad 90th
   12     0.61%        0.49%        0.54%           0.75%
   15     0.80%        0.70%        0.90%           1.22%
   20     1.10%        1.05%        1.28%           1.72%
   25     1.41%        1.40%        1.69%           2.25%
   30     1.71%        1.75%        2.12%           2.81%
   35     2.01%        2.10%        2.57%           3.39%
   40     2.32%        2.45%        3.04%           4.00%
   50     2.93%        3.15%        4.05%           5.30%

Analysis:
  - The TOS script uses a higher floor (8 vs 5) combined with a constant
    offset (+0.37%), which inflates the band at low VIX. This is a design
    choice for trading: wider bands = fewer false breakouts.
  - The TOS slope (0.0609) is ~13% flatter than our data-derived slope
    (0.0700), so it underpredicts at high VIX.
  - The TOS script contains ~65% of daily moves -- roughly equivalent to
    our linear 75th percentile model.
  - Our quadratic 75th percentile is the best apples-to-apples replacement:
    similar containment at normal VIX, but widens faster at high VIX to
    account for vol-of-vol.

RECOMMENDED REPLACEMENT FOR TOS SCRIPT:
  Quadratic 75th (2004+):
    move% = 0.1953 + 0.0659*(VIX-5) + 0.000440*(VIX-5)²

  For TOS implementation:
    move = 0.001953 + 0.000659*(VIX-5) + 0.00000440*(VIX-5)²
    top  = open * (1 + move)
    bottom = open * (1 - move)
